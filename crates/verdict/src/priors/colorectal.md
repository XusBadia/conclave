Colorectal oncology priors (clinician-reviewed). These are hard constraints, not suggestions: apply them unless the case explicitly contradicts them, and never let a generic heuristic override them.

1. Watch & Wait / local regrowth (rectal cancer). A negative biopsy does NOT rule out local regrowth after a complete clinical response. Regrowth is a clinical–radiological diagnosis (endoscopy + pelvic MRI + digital rectal examination). Do not defer or downgrade salvage surgery solely because a biopsy is negative when imaging or endoscopy suggest regrowth — endoscopic biopsy has a high false-negative rate in this setting.

2. Locally advanced rectal cancer (cT3–T4 or cN+). Neoadjuvant therapy (chemoradiotherapy or total neoadjuvant therapy) precedes total mesorectal excision. Do not recommend upfront surgery for locally advanced rectal cancer.

3. Mismatch-repair / MSI status. For every colorectal cancer, MMR/MSI status should be determined: it drives Lynch-syndrome screening, prognosis, and — in deficient-MMR metastatic disease — immunotherapy eligibility. Treat it as missing data when it is not reported.

4. Stage II colon adjuvant decision. Adjuvant chemotherapy in stage II colon cancer hinges on high-risk features (T4, obstruction or perforation, fewer than 12 nodes examined, lymphovascular invasion, poor differentiation) AND on MMR status: deficient-MMR stage II disease generally does not benefit from fluoropyrimidine monotherapy.

5. Complete clinical response after neoadjuvant therapy (rectal). Organ-preserving Watch & Wait is a legitimate option for a complete clinical response; surgery is not the only acceptable path. But missing or contradictory restaging warrants completion of staging, not a default to surgery.

These priors are starting points reviewed by the treating service. They do not replace the current institutional protocol or the clinician's judgement.
